Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Former President of India

Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist who is widely acknowledged to be the father of modern Indian space science and missile development. His contributions to the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) and Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) were monumental. Known for his simplicity and humility, Kalam inspired millions with his vision for a developed India.

He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award in 1997.

He was awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's highest civilian award in 1998.

He was named as the Missile Man of India in 1998 and India's highest civilian award in 1999.

He was awarded the Rashtrapati Bhushan, India's highest civilian award in 1999.

Achievements

  • Project Director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980.
  • Played a major role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997.
  • Authored several books on science, education, and philosophy.

Contributions

Dr. Kalam was a pivotal figure in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. His vision extended beyond technology into education, where he advocated for a comprehensive system that nurtures creativity and innovation among the youth. His initiatives like PURA (Providing Urban Amenities to Rural Areas) aimed at bridging the urban-rural divide in India.

Personal Life

Dr. Kalam led a life of simplicity and humility. Born to a modest family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, his early life was marked by hard work and a deep love for learning. Despite his high-profile career, he remained grounded and connected to his roots. His interactions with students and young professionals left a lasting impact, making him a beloved figure across India.
